It started with a phishing email. An employee clicked a malicious link, and within minutes, malware was installed on their workstation. The malware established a connection to a Command & Control (C2) server and began exfiltrating sensitive data.
But this wasn't your typical smash-and-grab operation. The attackers were clever - they used a covert channel technique to slowly exfiltrate data, hoping to blend in with normal network traffic. They knew that security teams monitor for large data transfers, so they went low and slow.
The data was broken down into individual characters and sent one byte at a time, hidden within seemingly innocent network packets. Among the noise of ARP broadcasts, ICMP pings, DNS queries, and web browsing traffic, the malicious packets flew under the radar.
Until now.
